The Problem

Beautiful Prototypes That Cannot Scale

Vibe coding has democratized web development. A grandmother can describe her bakery website to an AI assistant and get a beautiful, functional prototype in minutes. The colors are warm. The layout is inviting. The croissant photos render perfectly on her laptop screen.

Then she wants to grow. She needs online ordering, a loyalty program, a second location with its own menu. And the entire site needs to be rebuilt from scratch—because there are no specifications, no architecture, no reproducible system underneath the prototype. The AI generated code, not a plan.

The gap is not between humans and code. It is between human intent and machine-precise specifications.

On the other side of the spectrum, agentic engineers—the developers building AI-powered systems—spend more than half their time writing specifications that are ambiguous, incomplete, and require constant clarification. "Make it responsive" means something different to every developer. "Professional blue" could be any of fifty hex values. Every AI coding tool is focused on generating code, but code from ambiguous specs produces ambiguous results.

The industry has a missing layer: a tool that translates what humans mean into what machines need to know, with near-zero room for misinterpretation.

The Solution

Hey Bradley: A Specification Platform

Hey Bradley is a digital whiteboard where anyone—from that grandmother with her bakery to a lead agentic engineer shipping enterprise SaaS—speaks or types what they want, and the system creates near-zero-ambiguity enterprise-grade specifications. Not just a website builder. A specification platform.

The user describes a vision. Hey Bradley captures that intent and produces two outputs simultaneously: a live visual preview that proves the system understood the request, and six enterprise-grade specification documents that any developer, AI agent, or CI/CD pipeline can use to build production software without guesswork.

Key Insight

The preview is proof. The specs are the product. What the user sees is validation; what they download is value.

The pipeline is deliberate. Human intent enters the system as natural language. The AISP engine formalizes that intent into structured, unambiguous notation. From that notation, the system generates a live preview alongside full specifications: design tokens, component architecture, accessibility requirements, responsive breakpoints, content strategy, and deployment configuration. Each specification document is immediately consumable by Claude Code or any agentic coding system to produce production-ready software.

The Innovation

AISP: AI Symbolic Protocol

At the foundation of Hey Bradley is AISP—the AI Symbolic Protocol—created by Bradley Ross. AISP is a formal notation system that converts human and machine concepts into accurate mathematical frameworks via a fractal architecture. Where natural language leaves room for interpretation, AISP leaves room for none.

Crystal Atom: ⟦Ω, Σ, Γ, Λ, Ε⟧

The Crystal Atom notation encodes five dimensions of meaning: ontology (Ω), structure (Σ), grammar (Γ), logic (Λ), and evaluation (Ε). Every concept—from "make it responsive" to "add a shopping cart"—is decomposed into these five facets, eliminating the ambiguity that plagues natural language specifications.

Ambiguity Reduction by Specification Method

The research results have been striking. Testing across multiple domains shows that AISP reduces specification ambiguity from the 40–65% range typical of natural language down to less than 2% at the Platinum tier—a reduction that changes the economics of software development.

Research Findings

SWE-bench Verified 500 Verified

Achieved a 92% pass rate on 60 representative instances using AISP as a learning system. This demonstrates AISP can provide the mathematical framework for knowledge representation and function as a white-box explainable AI (XAI) system.

No Hints Used Verified

The original SWE-bench tests included developer hints valuable for solving tasks but not permitted in official submissions. AISP's XAI transparency means Bradley can re-run with full explainability to prove no forbidden hints were used—a level of auditability most AI systems cannot offer.

Zero Ambiguity vs. Creativity Research

Testing showed that eliminating ambiguity does not eliminate creativity—it channels it. The mathematical framework constrains interpretation, not imagination. Designers and developers report feeling more creative when freed from specification guesswork.

Fractal Design Architecture Research

AISP converts human and machine concepts into accurate mathematical frameworks via a fractal architecture. This enables a deterministic approach to AI—repeatable, auditable, and predictable outputs from identical inputs.

Knowledge Ingestion at Scale Active

Currently uploading all knowledge claims from Wikidata, PubMed, and Wikipedia. The goal: create a deterministic AI system grounded in verified, formalized human knowledge rather than probabilistic language model outputs.

51 Rust Crates Verified

51 completed Rust crates implementing the AISP framework, demonstrating potential to reduce LLM usage by 75%. Deterministic computation replaces probabilistic inference for routine specification tasks.

Open Source + Private Intent Research

Many methods are open-sourced via aisp-open-core, enabling community development. The intent system—AISP intent routing—remains private, representing the core intellectual property that captures why someone is building, not just what they want.

The Story

In Bradley's Words

When I started this project, I had a simple question: what if AI could write specifications, not just code? I had spent years watching developers waste time on ambiguous requirements. The specs said "make it responsive" but did not say what responsive meant for this specific layout. Every AI coding tool was focused on generating code—but code from ambiguous specs produces ambiguous results.

I created AISP because I needed a notation system where "responsive" has exactly one interpretation: breakpoints at 375px, 768px, 1440px with specific column collapse rules. Where "professional blue" means #1e40af, not whatever the LLM imagines that day.

The open-core approach lets the community build on the base protocol while I develop the intent layer—the part that captures why someone is building a site, not just what they want. That intent layer is what separates a spec tool from an AI architect.

After the capstone, I am pushing further. The SWE-bench results showed AISP can learn from codebases. The Wikidata ingestion shows it can formalize any domain's knowledge. And the Rust crates show we can make this fast enough to replace most LLM calls for routine tasks.

Hey Bradley is the first application. But AISP is the platform.

Development

Built by Agents, Guided by Humans

Hey Bradley was not just built with AI tools—it was built by AI agents under human orchestration. Each phase deployed 30+ specialized Claude Code agents working in parallel: coders, reviewers, testers, security auditors, and quality assessors. The development methodology itself is an innovation.

The process follows a 3-loop optimization cycle: agents build, agents review with brutal honesty, and agents refine based on scored feedback. Human oversight gates every phase transition. Phase 8 deployed more than 30 agents in a single day, coordinating across template generation, test coverage, accessibility audits, and specification validation.

Roadmap

What Comes Next

The capstone demonstrates the core concept. What follows is a deliberate expansion from proof-of-concept to platform, each stage adding capabilities while maintaining the specification-first architecture.

Stage 5 — Enterprise
Multi-Page, Marketplace, White Label, API
Full enterprise platform: multi-page site specifications, template marketplace, white-label licensing, and a public API for third-party integrations.
