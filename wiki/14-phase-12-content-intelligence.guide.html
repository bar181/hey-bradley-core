What Phase 12 Changes

Phase 12 adds the intelligence layer. Instead of generating the same generic specs for every project, Hey Bradley will understand purpose (marketing vs. portfolio vs. SaaS), audience (consumer vs. developer vs. enterprise), and tone (formal vs. casual vs. playful). Every spec generator produces different output based on these choices. A fun food blog gets different content than a Fortune 500 SaaS product page.

🧠 Site Context System PLANNED

The core Phase 12 feature. A new SiteContext interface adds structured metadata to every project:

Context Fields

  • Purpose — marketing, portfolio, saas, blog, agency, restaurant, ecommerce
  • Audience — consumer, business, developer, enterprise, creative
  • Tone — formal, casual, playful, technical, warm, bold
  • Content Guidelines — max words per section, visual density, CTA style
  • Brand Guidelines — brand name, tagline, voice attributes, words to avoid

How It Feeds Into Specs

  • North Star generator uses purpose + audience to shape the vision statement
  • Build Plan uses tone + content guidelines to set copy length and CTA approach
  • AISP Crystal Atom encodes context as Lambda bindings for machine consumption
  • All 6 spec generators produce audience-appropriate output
