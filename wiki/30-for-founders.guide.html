📖 The Story Every Founder Knows

You have a vision. Maybe it's a landing page for a new product. Maybe it's an investor-facing site before your Series A. Maybe it's a recruiting page that doesn't make engineers cringe. You can see it in your head — clean hero, three benefits, social proof, a single call-to-action. You could describe it in five sentences over coffee.

So you call your designer. Two weeks later you have wireframes. The wireframes are wrong because you forgot to mention the brand voice. Three more weeks pass. You have a Figma file. Your developer says it'll take another four weeks because the spec leaves twelve things ambiguous. By the time the site goes live, your launch window has closed and your CMO is asking why a static page took two months and forty-three thousand dollars.

"It feels like every time I want to ship a website, I have to translate my idea through three people who each lose 20% of it."

Here's the part nobody says out loud: most marketing sites are 70% solved problems. Hero, blog, pricing, testimonials, FAQ, footer. Same fifteen sections, recombined. The "design" isn't the bottleneck. The specification is — that messy translation from "what you mean" to "what an engineer can build."

Hey Bradley fixes the bottleneck. You describe what you want — by typing or by talking — and it produces two things at once: a live preview you can watch update in real time, AND a JSON specification document so precise that Claude Code (or any modern AI dev agent) can build the production site directly from it. No designer-developer game of telephone. No 12-page brief that still has gaps.

The trick is something called AISP — a math-first symbolic protocol that gives the AI a near-zero ambiguity input. Where a normal natural-language brief leaves an LLM guessing 30% of the time, an AISP spec leaves it guessing under 2%. Your "voice" gets compressed into a structure that doesn't lose meaning.

The result: the same idea, the same five-sentence pitch, takes 30 seconds instead of two months. You walk away with a working preview, a spec your dev team can ship, and an AI build-out path that costs less than the catered lunch from your last design review.

🎯 What Your Day Looks Like

  1. Open Hey Bradley on your laptop. Pick a vibe ("Professional", "Warm", "Bold"). The preview snaps to that style instantly.
  2. Talk or type what you want. "Build me a SaaS landing page with pricing and testimonials." The site assembles in front of you.
  3. Iterate by saying it differently. "Make the hero more bold." "Hide the blog section." "Change the headline to 'Stop guessing, start shipping.'" Each change applies in seconds.
  4. Bradley shows his work. A small panel under each reply shows what he heard, what he assumed, and which template he used. If he's unsure, he gives you 3 buttons to pick the closest match — no silent guessing.
  5. Export the JSON specification + preview. Hand it to your AI build agent (Claude Code, Cursor, etc). Your production site is live by tomorrow morning.

🎯 What This Is Not

We're honest about scope. Hey Bradley covers marketing sites — landing pages, agency portfolios, SaaS pages, founder/personal sites, capstone-grade research sites. It is intentionally NOT:

  • An e-commerce platform (Shopify wins this)
  • A blog CMS (WordPress / Ghost win this)
  • A web app builder (Bubble / Retool win this)
  • A replacement for your designer if you need bespoke brand systems

It feeds your AI dev agent. It doesn't replace your business strategy. It collapses the spec-to-code timeline from weeks to a coffee break.
